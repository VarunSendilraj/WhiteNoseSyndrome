What is It?

The identified causative agent of White-Nose Syndrome (WNS), Pseudogymnoascus destructans, has been responsible for the mortality of an estimated 6.7 million North American bats since its emergence in 2006. Click here to learn more

The plan

The antifungal properties of Rhodococcus rhodochrous strain DAP96253 had a major effect on the growth of PD. By isolating the genes that cause the metabolic pathway (NHase, amidase, and nitrilase), the process can be mimicked in an e-coli chassis.

The Design

Firstly, a T7 inducible promoter (Developed my Lambert igem) is commonly used to produce high levels of transcription for proteins. This promoter also works well with a T7 RNAP, so BL21(DE3) should be used. Next, a strong ribosome site is needed so part BBa_B0034 is used. This RBS that works well with protein expression and it will be used with a protein coding sequence that should be developed by our team using the blast of the enzymes genes. Finally, a bidirectional terminator (BBa_B1001) should be used. This keeps the construct as compact as possible, while providing proper termination.

Whats Next

This new e-coli should be duplicated multiple times, until it is made into an antifungal spray. Then using machine learning, the next predicted outbreak should be identified. Then a WNS team can go it and spray the caves with the new antifungal destructans.
